About the library.
The Internet Public Library is a free, web-based research and reference platform that consolidates structured information from a wide range of external sources which include public APIs, cultural institutions, and community-maintained wikis. The system ingests heterogeneous data, reorganizes it, and renders it through a single page application.
  • For artists, it provides high-quality visual references.
  • For musicians, it offers playback utilities and theory tools.
  • For general use, it enables exploration of curated datasets across media, culture, and science.
With the library's structured data, its use serves to reduce the amount of chaos in reference seeking by adding on opportunities for learning more about what is being looked at through said data.
Subject Matter

Datasets integrated into the library are limited only by their data quality*. Beyond that standard, subject matter of any kind is welcome. New sources are continually evaluated and incorporated during development. Some datasets are exceptionally large—spanning millions of records collected over decades—such as those from the Smithsonian or NASA. The library presents records in a way which surfaces the world’s buried information and increases access to it.

* High-quality data is media-rich, complete or sufficiently complete in its metadata, and consistent in its structure.

Layout Considerations

Each dataset in the library is presented through a consistent two to three-panel layout: left column, main panel, and a potential third panel. This familiar structure provides a stable interface while still allowing each implementation to adapt to the unique qualities of its data source. For example, an art-museum dataset may use the left column for filtering options and the right panel for browsing results. A dataset derived from a MediaWiki source may instead use the left column as a table of contents (which does not exist in Wiki sites), with the right panel dedicated to the article data. In cases where both filtering and hierarchical navigation are required, a third panel is added for query results. This system allows the library to accommodate any of its data structures while maintaining familiarity.

Careful attention is given to preventing layout shift across the interface. When panels contain extensive content, scrollbars are applied selectively so that the left and right panels remain aligned and visible together. Additional optimizations such as dynamic-panel-sizing ensure stability in areas that would otherwise cause significant movement or resizing during interaction. The combination of science-fiction–inspired CSS and layout management contributes to the comfort in the library’s user interface.
