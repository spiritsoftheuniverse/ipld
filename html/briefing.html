What is the library?
Internet Public Library is an online establishment for reference and research that is free for world to use. It presents abundant and organized information through stylized page design with visual cues taken from science-fiction. Information is retrieved from numerous organizations and counting, ranging anywhere from art institutions to fan-made wikis. The data is collected, organized, and presented in formatting that is made most efficient through bespoke layout considerations per implementation. Each dataset can potentially be very large with millions of records throughout the decades such as those found at The Smithsonian and Nasa.
Subject matter and quality.

Datasets that are implemented into the library are limited only by data quality. Subject matter of all kinds are otherwise welcome and scouting for new data happens regularly during development. Quality refers to media-richness, adequate completeness, and reliable data-structure. These 3 pillars make any dataset adequate for implementation.

The library is meant to have breadth and abundance of knowledge, being an act of stewardship in the process of development and provider of information at grand-scale as a minimum.

Doing the right thing right now
While the future is bright, there is so much in the now which can be focused on. The amount of underused libraries for web applications is rather large due to the fact that we undervalue our own output in favor of the media consolidation which harbors most of the world's attention, being the social-media applications whom all of the world are plugged into. The library stands as an establishment which offers everything that the world outside of academia does not do very freely, and that is be the best it can be at what it serves at no cost to the people who use it.
