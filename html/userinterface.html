Theme
The library's user-interface is rendered in css and html, serving the look of space-age frosted panels and distinct border-lines as its theme. Graphical personality and functionality are both working together. Such visual design has been abandoned since the release of Windows Vista and has rarely been pursued on the web at all throughout the ages of css.
Page Structure
Most implementations have similar page structure as a base for further navigation. The most common page structure is of two panels on top and one full length panel below the both of them. The top sequence of panels consists of a left column that is roughly 200 pixels wide with another panel beside it that takes up the rest of the horizontal space. In smaller widths, the left panel is responsive and will collapse into a list which becomes set away from view in fixed position. For some datasets, the left column is populated with filtration toggles while the right panel is populated with category links, and the bottom panel is dedicated to rendering query results and potentially a gallery view. For other datasets, the page structure is simplified to having only the top two panels. Mediawiki implementations are like this because the left column is dedicated to table of contents navigation with the right panel being where page and category results are rendered.
